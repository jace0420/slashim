# SLASHIM

SLASHIM is a text-based narrative procedural generation simulator akin to classic websites such as [brantsteele.net](https://brantsteele.net/).

The simulation takes the form of a classic slasher flick where the user creates the cast members and killer, chooses the locations, edits other details, and lets the simulation run by watching the characters and killer interact.

Rather than a randomly pulled, tension-increasing text randomizer, SLASHIM is an actual persistent world space that uses each character's needs, desires, goals, and survival instincts to drive their actions and movement throughout the world. Characters can interact with objects, move between rooms, converse with each other, and more.

---

## Core Simulation

Survivors must gather supplies — food, water, equipment, and more. At first, they will be unaware of the presence of a killer. As the killer does what their name suggests, tension rises and it becomes clear to the survivors that they must escape.

All survivors have distinct personalities. Some may try to help others, while some save only themselves. Some may try to fight, and some may try to hide or run away.

Survivors and the killer both have five senses and memory. This means that if somebody is killed secretly, none of the other survivors will know a killer is present. However, if the killer has a personality that compels them to display the body openly, all survivors will know immediately.

---

## Character Systems

How the user edits the cast drastically changes how the story unfolds. Character stats influence behavior, decision-making, and outcomes:

- **Sanity** — affects decision quality and stability under pressure
- **Relationships** — shapes alliances, betrayals, and cooperative behavior
- **Fear** — influences fight-or-flight responses and risk tolerance
- **Adrenaline** — drives sporadic, chaotic, or heightened actions

---

## Design Goals

SLASHIM aims to be the most advanced horror simulator in existence, featuring realistic AI-driven (not generative) characters and a fully persistent text-based world space.
